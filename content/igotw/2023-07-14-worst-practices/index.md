I've recently become enamored with [Worst Practices - a YouTube channel (and ](https://www.youtube.com/@WorstPractices)[podcast](https://www.youtube.com/@WorstPractices/about)) of brief (4-5 minute) interviews with developers sharing things they do that are commonly considered "sub-optimal" in industry. Covering things from [eschewing modern IDE features to ](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rysgxl35EGc)[not reading documentation](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=krMw1QTP2no), every one of the videos to date covers something I've either done in the past or, in fact, [still do.](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XL4XTr23b8U)

In an industry rife with _imposter syndrome_, I love the humanity of a channel dedicated to demonstrating that yes, there are engineers out there - good, seasoned engineers! - who are still [debugging with print() statements](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NSscglJfrTc)... just like me.
