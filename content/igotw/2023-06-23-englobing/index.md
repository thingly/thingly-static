TIL: Wiktionary defines **englobing** as the present participle of [englobe, meaning "to surround as if by a globe". It also notes that this term is used almost ](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/englobe#English) [exclusively in the context of phagocytosis, but I'm mostly just tickled that it's an anagram of ](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/phagocytosis)[**belonging**](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/belonging)[. I'm no poet, but to me this is about as linguistically ](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/belonging) euphonious as it gets.

To what do you "belong"?

[What tribe, what family, what set or genus makes you think "People like ](https://seths.blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/2017-people-like-us.pdf)[*us*](https://seths.blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/2017-people-like-us.pdf)[ do things like ](https://seths.blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/2017-people-like-us.pdf)[*this*](https://seths.blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/2017-people-like-us.pdf)[.](https://seths.blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/2017-people-like-us.pdf)"?

Makes you feel seen?

Makes you feel - I daresay - englobed?

Or perhaps your vision of yourself is that of the englob  - the the clan-maker, the community-builder, the guild officer - the extender of pseudopodia, *er* drawing people into the vacuoles of your cytoplasm?

Err...so to speak.

Happy Belonging inDay, folks.
